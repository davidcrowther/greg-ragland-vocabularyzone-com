The most popular and commercially successful "vocabulary building"
methods are too slow and complex to be effective.

We've all heard that you have to read more if you want to improve your vocabulary. Although true, you've probably found it's not the most efficient method.

Quick question: how have you already tried to improve your vocabulary in the past?

  • Signing up to a series of "Word of the day" emails?
  • Writing down a new word every time you hear it?
  • Thumbing through a thesaurus or looking online to find the word? (too slow and impractical)

Well the truth is ... You Have A Better Vocabulary Than You Think

The most important part of improving your vocabulary is NOT using
"paralogize" one day and "salubrious" the next.

It's using words that are in your audience's passive vocabulary.

If you have an above average intelligence, you likely already have an expansive passive vocabulary.

The problem is that these words don't come to mind when you need them: while you are talking to someone important, like the company CEO, or when you are discussing current events with people you’re trying to impress.

Less Is More

Having a big vocabulary does not mean using long words or speaking a lot - that's a myth.

Communicating well and having a powerful vocabulary is about using just the right word at the right time.

The more precise your vocabulary, the more clearly you will communicate.

If you want all those interesting, precise words at the front of your brain when you need them, you need to move them from your passive vocabulary to your active vocabulary.
