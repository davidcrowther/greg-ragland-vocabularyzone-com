Vocabulary Makes The Difference

Research by the Johnson O'Conner Foundation found a direct
correlation between the size of a person's vocabulary and their
rank on the corporate ladder.

What that means is ...
The one unifying factor amongst successful people
is that they are all great communicators.

Get The Life Changing Power
Of A Strong Vocabulary Without Using
Rote-Memorization

The most popular and commercially successful "vocabulary building"
methods are too slow and complex to be effective.

We've all heard that you have to read more if you want to improve your vocabulary. Although true, you've probably found it's not the most efficient method.

Let me ask you, how have you already tried to improve your vocabulary in the past?

  • Writing down a new word every time you hear it?
  • Signing up to a series of "Word of the day" emails?
  • Thumbing through a thesaurus or looking online to find the word? (too slow and impractical)

Well the truth is ... You Have A Better Vocabulary Than You Think

The most important part of improving your vocabulary is not using
"paralogize" one day and "salubrious" the next.

It's using words that are in your audience's passive vocabulary.

If you have an above average intelligence, you likely already have an expansive passive vocabulary.

The problem is that these words don't come to mind when you need them: while you are talking to the
company director or when you are discussing current events with friends.

Less Is More

Having a big vocabulary does not
mean using long words
or speaking a lot - that's a myth.

Communicating well and having a
powerful vocabulary is about using
just the right word at the right time.

The more precise your vocabulary, the more
clearly you will communicate.

If you want all those interesting, precise words at
the front of your brain when you need them, you
need to move them from your passive vocabulary to
your active vocabulary.
